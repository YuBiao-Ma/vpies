Teaser: Robust locomotion across stairs, gravel, obstacles, and external pulls with zero privileged inputs at deployment.

📌Abstract

In complex environments under partial observability, legged robots, especially humanoids, remain significant challenges. Legged locomotion via reinforcement learning is often framed as a teacher-student paradigm, where a proprioceptive student policy imitates a teacher trained with privileged inputs. While effective for bootstrapping, this pipeline can induce representation mismatch and covariate shift, leaving a gap at deployment that ultimately constrains real-world generalization. Inspired by human learning, where teachers provide scaffold that are gradually faded rather than demanding pure imitation, we propose Variational Privileged-Information Encoder as Scaffold (VPIES), a single-stage RL framework that learns temporally structured proprioceptive representations with short-long encoders while encoding privileged states into a KL-regularized latent that serves as a training-time scaffold. An adaptive KL schedule gradually removes dependence on privilege, enabling privilege-free deployment by replacing the latent with a zero vector. In simulation, VPIES reduces velocity-tracking error by approximately 20% and improves out-of-distribution robustness by approximately 20% on quadrupeds, and yields stable, agile behaviors on humanoids in complex terrains.

Training Method

VPIES method diagram

VPIES as a training-time scaffold for actor-side exploration. Privileged states st (red; simulation only) are encoded into a variational latent zt and concatenated with proprioceptive history features from the short/long encoders (blue; available in both simulation and the real world). The actor is trained with zt plus proprioception. During deployment, the scaffold module is removed and the policy runs privilege-free, enabling zero-shot sim-to-real transfer.

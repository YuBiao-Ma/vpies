üìåAbstract

In complex environments under partial observability, legged robots, especially humanoids, remain significant challenges. Legged locomotion via reinforcement learning is often framed as a teacher-student paradigm, where a proprioceptive student policy imitates a teacher trained with privileged inputs. While effective for bootstrapping, this pipeline suffers from representation misalignment, covariate shift, and weak deployable adaptation, which limits real-world generalization. Inspired by human learning, where teachers provide scaffold that are gradually faded rather than demanding pure imitation, we propose Variational Privileged-Information Encoder as Scaffold (VPIES), a single-stage RL framework that learns temporally structured proprioceptive representations with short-long encoders while encoding privileged states into a KL-regularized latent that serves as a training-time scaffold. An adaptive KL schedule gradually removes dependence on privilege, enabling zero-privilege deployment by replacing the latent with a zero vector. In simulation, VPIES reduces velocity-tracking error by approximately 20% and improves out-of-distribution robustness by approximately 30% on quadrupeds, and yields stable, agile behaviors on humanoids in complex terrains.
